Mentor summary (1–2 minute read)

Progress snapshot: I can now explain crypto systems as layered components instead of isolated buzzwords. I moved from “asset-first thinking” to “protocol-first thinking”: network trust model, identity/authorization, transaction flow, then app-level execution.

Conceptual sequence mastered: decentralization/transparency/immutability → blockchain structure → key management and signatures → mempool and block inclusion through fee markets and consensus → Ethereum-specific execution via EVM and gas.

Practical competency: I can read explorer entries, reason about pending vs confirmed status, distinguish coin vs token, and articulate when on-chain vs off-chain handling is appropriate. I can also explain p2p and OTC exchange choices in terms of trade size, counterparty risk, and settlement verification.

Risk awareness: I understand that wallet security is primarily an operational discipline centered on seed phrase protection and signing hygiene. The biggest gap in beginners is often process quality, not technical vocabulary.

Suggested next mentoring focus: transaction simulation tooling, deeper consensus tradeoffs across networks, and case-based review of smart contract failure modes.
